Saudi Red Crescent Authority

An association was established in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia under the name of the Saudi Red Crescent Society, pursuant to Royal Decree No. 1 of 1/16/1383 AH. It has legal status for an unlimited period, and its main headquarters is the city of Riyadh. Its activities include all parts of the Kingdom, and its system is based on the Geneva Conventions and the principles approved by conferences. The International Red Crescent and the Red Cross, and the name of the association was changed to the Saudi Red Crescent Authority by Cabinet Resolution No. 371 dated 12/24/1429 AH.

About Saudi Red Crescent

In the year 1354 AH, the idea of ​​establishing a charitable ambulance in the Kingdom was born,
as the first government organization to provide emergency medical service in the Kingdom.
After that, Royal Order No. 3306 was issued on 2-3-1354 AH, corresponding to 4-6-1935 AD, during the reign
of King Faisal bin Abdulaziz, establishing the first independent body to To provide emergency medical service
in the Kingdom, under the name (Charitable Ambulance Society).
